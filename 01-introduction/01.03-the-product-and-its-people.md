# The Product and Its People

## The product

EduFlow is an online learning platform. Instructors upload courses made of video lessons; students pay to enroll and then watch those lessons, track their progress, and receive a certificate when they complete a course. An admin oversees the platform — approving courses before they go live, reviewing flagged content, and monitoring platform-wide metrics.

The product's core loop is:

> Instructor creates a course → Admin reviews and publishes it → Student browses the catalogue and enrolls → Student watches lessons and marks them complete → Student earns a certificate

Every feature you build lives inside that loop. If a feature doesn't serve that loop, it's probably out of scope.

## The actors

### Instructor

An instructor is a person with domain knowledge who wants to teach it. They sign up, verify their email, and request instructor access (which an admin approves). Once approved, they can:

- Create and publish courses with a title, description, cover image, price, and category
- Add video lessons to a course and arrange them in a sequence
- See how many students are enrolled in each of their courses
- See per-lesson completion rates

An instructor cannot see other instructors' courses in the admin view, and cannot access student data beyond the aggregate counts on their own courses.

### Student

A student is anyone learning on the platform. They sign up, verify their email, and browse the catalogue freely. When they enroll in a course:

- They gain access to all lessons in that course
- Their progress is tracked per lesson — watched or not
- When all lessons are complete, a certificate is generated and available for download

A student cannot access lessons in a course they are not enrolled in — not by URL manipulation, not by guessing IDs. This constraint is enforced on the server, not just the UI.

### Admin

An admin is a platform operator. There is at least one admin seeded at deploy time. Admins can:

- Approve or reject courses submitted by instructors
- Deactivate user accounts
- View platform-wide metrics: total users, total enrollments, revenue by course
- Access the admin dashboard

An admin cannot enroll in courses or submit courses as an instructor (roles are separate).

## The request-response loop in one line

> Student browses catalogue → clicks a course → enrolls (pays) → watches lessons → marks lessons complete → downloads certificate.
